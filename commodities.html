Commodities

This digital project focuses on the commodities exported from Africa to Europe during the nineteenth and twentieth century.

The periodization of the dataset falls between the British abolition of the slave trade in 1807 and World War II. The dataset, like this website, is concerned with two periods in African and European history: so-called legitimate trade in the nineteenth century and the colonial period in the early twentieth. The term legitimate trade signaled a shift in the focus of European trading engagements with Africa from enslaved persons to commodities. However, there was no clean break between these two periods. Slaves continued to be shipped across the Atlantic and Indian Ocean in the nineteenth century.

The colonial period continued some export trends from the era of legitimate trade. Commodities like peanuts, for example, increased in export quantities during the colonial period. Others like gum and resins maintained consistent export quantities during the colonial period and did not continue to increase in quantity as they had during the era of legitimate trade.

The essays below focus on single commodity exports from different regions on the African continent. Each essay discusses the historical context and explores the available data in light of changing production processes in Africa and Europe. The data for this digital project comes from the African Commodity Trade Database (ACTD).
